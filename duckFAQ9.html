Some clients have salt water pools.
Some have Koi ponds.
Some have kiddie pools.
Some have chlorinated pools.
Some have hot tubs.
First question is: do you want duck manure in your pool? They poop every 15 minutes.
Second is: will this be a good environment for the duckling? Chlorine is not good for a duckling. So we would say big NO to that.

Ducks eat fish.
In fact, you can buy little feeder fish and put them in a kiddie pool to watch the ducks swim and try to catch them if you’re up to it! 
We’d say NO to the Koi pond for the sake of the fish.

Bathtubs, kiddie pools, basins and kitchen sinks are great swimming places 
BUT ALWAYS SUPERVISED and never for too long as THEY WILL GET WATERLOGGED.
It’s a good idea to dry off with a hair dryer on low to get their body temperature back up for the first week. Later, as ducks get bigger it’s not so much an issue.

There is a little oil glad on the duck’s back near the tail. The motion of a duck rubbing it’s beak in this area promotes the secretion of oil and the spread it over their feathers to waterproof.
