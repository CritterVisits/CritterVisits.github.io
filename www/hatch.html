The Waiting Game Eggs go into the prepared incubator
Day One Eggs will automatically roll (turn) several times a day so your role is easy. You will keep an eye on the humidity meter and add water as needed. The computerized incubator does the rest. Our instruction guide includes information how the incubator works, shows development of the egg, has illustrations on candling eggs and what to expect to see. We include a customized timeline; from eggs-in to hatching and brooding your chicks/ducks, before they return to our farm.
Candling lets you discover which eggs are viable and should hatch. You can see the "spider web" at 5 days by beaming the light up through the shell. You may also be able to see the heart beating through the shell of a viable egg at day 8. We use candling to verify if eggs have developed as hatch day approaches and to remove eggs that will not hatch.

LOCKDOWN is the last incubator step prior to hatch day and marks the time for you to get the brooder box ready.
When you follow our directions using our eggs and kit, we guarantee a hatch every time. Clients keep the hatched chicks/ducklings for about 11 days beyond the predicted hatch date. All hatchlings must return to the farm at the end of this program per our contract.
